## Certified in Cybersecurity (CC) — ISC2

### Purpose

- Build a structured foundation in cybersecurity principles
- Understand common security domains and baseline practices

### Topics Covered

- Security principles
- Business continuity and disaster recovery
- Access control
- Network security
- Security operations

### Practical Relevance

This certification provided a baseline for later governance, risk, and technical security learning.

### Personal Takeaway

Cybersecurity fundamentals remain important even when moving into management, assurance, or GRC roles.

### Topics Covered

- Security architecture and design
- Identity and access management
- Security operations
- Incident response
- Governance, risk, and compliance

### Practical Relevance

Security+ helped consolidate broad cybersecurity concepts and created a useful base for later work in GRC, audit, operations, and security management.

## ISO/IEC 27001 Lead Auditor

### Purpose

- Understand how information security management systems are audited
- Learn how controls, evidence, risk treatment, and continual improvement fit together

### Topics Covered

- ISMS principles
- Audit planning
- Audit evidence
- Nonconformities
- Risk treatment
- Management system improvement

### Practical Relevance

ISO/IEC 27001 is useful because it connects security controls to governance, accountability, risk ownership, evidence, and continual improvement.

### Personal Takeaway

An ISMS should not be treated as a one-off certification exercise. It should operate as a repeatable governance and assurance mechanism.

### Topics Covered

- Reconnaissance
- Scanning
- Enumeration
- Vulnerability analysis
- System hacking concepts
- Web application attacks
- Wireless and cloud security concepts
- Defensive thinking

### Practical Relevance

CEH helped bridge GRC and technical security by showing how weak controls can be abused in practice.

## Microsoft SC-401 — Information Security Administrator Associate

### Purpose

- Strengthen practical knowledge of Microsoft Purview and Microsoft 365 information protection controls
- Understand how data security, compliance, and governance controls are configured and operationalized in a Microsoft-native environment

### Topics Covered

- Sensitivity labels and information protection
- Data loss prevention
- Retention and records management concepts
- Insider risk management concepts
- Audit, alerting, and compliance workflows
- Microsoft Purview governance and administration

### Practical Relevance

SC-401 is relevant to organizations that need to protect sensitive information across Microsoft 365 services while balancing usability, governance, regulatory expectations, and operational evidence.

### Personal Takeaway

Information protection should not be treated as a standalone technical configuration. It should be designed around data sensitivity, business process, user behaviour, evidence requirements, and governance ownership.

## Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA) — ISACA

### Purpose

- Strengthen audit, assurance, and control evaluation skills
- Improve ability to assess whether systems and processes are properly governed and controlled

### Topics Covered

- IT audit process
- Governance and management of IT
- Information systems acquisition, development, and implementation
- Information systems operations and business resilience
- Protection of information assets

### Practical Relevance

CISA supports audit and assurance work by focusing on whether controls are appropriately designed, evidenced, and operating effectively.

### Personal Takeaway

Good assurance work should be evidence-based, risk-aligned, and actionable. A control is not mature simply because it exists; it must be properly designed, owned, performed, monitored, and evidenced.

## Project Management Professional (PMP) — PMI

### Purpose

- Strengthen project delivery, stakeholder management, planning, and governance
- Improve ability to lead cybersecurity, IT, compliance, and transformation initiatives

### Topics Covered

- Project initiation and planning
- Scope, schedule, cost, quality, and risk
- Stakeholder engagement
- Communication management
- Agile, predictive, and hybrid approaches
- Business value and delivery outcomes

### Practical Relevance

Cybersecurity work often depends on project execution: scoping, planning, communicating, tracking risks, managing stakeholders, and closing deliverables properly.

### Personal Takeaway

Cybersecurity work can fail even when the control is technically valid if the project is poorly scoped, poorly communicated, or poorly implemented.
