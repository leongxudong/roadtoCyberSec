# Knowledge Domains

This page organizes cybersecurity learning by capability area rather than by certification.

The purpose is to show how different certifications, work experience, and practical projects connect to real information security responsibilities.

## Domain Overview

| Domain | Focus | Practical Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Security Foundations | Core security principles, threats, architecture, and operations | Control checklists, awareness content, basic hardening notes |
| Governance, Risk, and Compliance | Frameworks, policies, risk assessment, audit, and compliance | Risk registers, policy mappings, control assessments |
| Security Assurance and Audit | Audit planning, evidence review, findings, and corrective actions | Audit checklists, CAP templates, evidence matrices |
| Vendor and Third-Party Risk | Vendor classification, due diligence, contract review, and assurance | Vendor assessment templates, risk rating models |
| Microsoft and Cloud Security | Identity, endpoint, compliance, Azure, and Microsoft 365 controls | Access review checklists, security configuration notes |
| Security Awareness and Human Risk | Staff guidance, phishing awareness, behaviour change, and scenario-based learning | SETA content, quizzes, microlearning materials |
| Incident Response and Resilience | Detection, escalation, triage, response, recovery, and business continuity | Escalation flows, incident playbooks, BIA notes |
| Project and Change Delivery | Planning, stakeholder management, implementation, and closure | Project charters, implementation plans, lessons learned |
| AI Governance and Emerging Risk | AI risk, acceptable use, privacy, accountability, and governance | AI use policy notes, risk assessment prompts, control mappings |

---

## Security Foundations

### Topics

- CIA triad
- Authentication and authorization
- Network and endpoint security
- Common attack techniques
- Vulnerability management
- Secure configuration
- Security operations basics

### Practical Focus

Strong fundamentals help make better governance and assurance decisions. Security policies and risk statements are more defensible when grounded in how systems, users, and attackers actually behave.

---

## Governance, Risk, and Compliance

### Topics

- ISO/IEC 27001 and ISMS governance
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- COBIT governance principles
- Risk assessment and treatment
- Security policies and standards
- PDPA and data protection considerations
- Cyber maturity assessment

### Practical Focus

GRC work should translate business, legal, regulatory, and technical risks into clear decisions, controls, ownership, and follow-up.

---

## Security Assurance and Audit

### Topics

- Audit planning and scope definition
- Evidence gathering
- Control design effectiveness
- Control operating effectiveness
- Risk-based findings
- Corrective action planning
- Management reporting

### Practical Focus

A useful audit finding should be clear, evidence-based, risk-aligned, and actionable. The goal is not fault-finding, but control reliability improvement.

---

## Vendor and Third-Party Risk

### Topics

- Vendor classification
- Data sensitivity and access review
- Cloud and SaaS risk
- Contract and SLA review
- Security due diligence
- Residual risk and business acceptance

### Practical Focus

Vendor assessment should be proportionate. The depth of review should depend on data sensitivity, system access, service criticality, regulatory exposure, and business dependency.

---

## Microsoft and Cloud Security

### Topics

- Microsoft 365 and Microsoft security architecture
- Microsoft Entra ID, Conditional Access, PIM, and workload identities
- Azure networking, compute, storage, databases, Key Vault, and Policy
- Microsoft Defender XDR, Defender for Cloud, Sentinel, and exposure management
- Microsoft Purview, Intune, and information protection
- Zero Trust, hybrid/multicloud governance, and shared responsibility

### Practical Focus

Cloud security architecture requires coordinated identity, network, workload, data, posture, monitoring, resilience, user-experience, and governance controls. The AZ-500 and SC-100 learning repositories turn these areas into practical implementation and design exercises.

---

## Security Awareness and Human Risk

### Topics

- Phishing awareness
- Scenario-based training
- Secure handling of information
- Password and authentication practices
- Reporting suspicious activity
- Non-technical security communication

### Practical Focus

Security awareness should help users make better decisions at the moment of risk. It should not be limited to annual training completion.

---

## Incident Response and Resilience

### Topics

- Event escalation
- Incident classification
- Triage and containment
- Communication and reporting
- Recovery coordination
- Lessons learned
- Business continuity and impact analysis

### Practical Focus

Incident response depends on role clarity, escalation paths, evidence preservation, business communication, and repeatable procedures.

---

## Project and Change Delivery

### Topics

- Project initiation
- Stakeholder management
- Scope and requirement gathering
- Risk and issue management
- Implementation planning
- Handover and closure

### Practical Focus

Cybersecurity initiatives are often delivered through projects. Security work must therefore be planned, communicated, tracked, and closed properly.

---

## AI Governance and Emerging Risk

### Topics

- AI acceptable use and lifecycle governance
- Data, identity, model, and prompt risks
- Human oversight and output validation
- Third-party AI, model, dataset, plugin, and supply-chain risk
- AI policy, impact assessment, control design, monitoring, and evidence
- Responsible AI laws, standards, roles, and accountability

### Practical Focus

AI governance should balance innovation and control. The main concern is not just tool adoption, but data handling, accountability, transparency, and risk ownership.
